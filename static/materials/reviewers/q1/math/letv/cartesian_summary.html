Cartesian Coordinate System And Lines

The Cartesian Coordinate System

  • The Y-Axis is the Vertical line, whilst the X-axis is the Horizontal line
  • Coordinates are understood by having the X-coordinate first, followed by the Y-coordinate. (As seen above)
  • (0,0) appears at the center.
  • Each Quadrant has different properties
    • Quadrant 1 - Positive X, Positive Y
    • Quadrant 2 - Negative X, Positive Y
    • Quadrant 3 - Negative X, Negative Y
    • Quadrant 4 - Positive X, Negative Y

Lines

  • Plotting the solutions for an equation and drawing a line is done by substituting a value to either X or Y and solving the equation to get either the corresponding Y or X value respectively.

Intercepts of a Line

A line can have at most two intercepts: X-Intercept and Y-Intercept

  • The X-Intercept is the coordinates where the line intersects or crosses the X-Axis
  • The Y-Intercept is the coordinates where the line intersects or crosses the Y-Axis

Without using a graph, we can find the the Y-intercept by substituting the X value with 0 and solving the Equation to get the Y value. The same can be done with the X-intercept.

Slope Of a Line

Note: x1,y1x_1,y_1x1​,y1​﻿ and x2,y2x_2, y_2x2​,y2​﻿ are two pairs of coordinates that you can find along a given line.

In other words, the slope of the line is found by how many units the Y value increases per 1 increase in the X-value.

For example, given (5, 5), and as the X value increased by one unit (6), the Y value then increases by 2 (7). Therefore, the slope of this given line is 2.
